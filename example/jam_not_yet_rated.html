GMTK Game Jam 2025

The GMTK Game Jam is an annual game making marathon, where individuals and teams try to make a game that fits a theme, in a super short time period.

Each year thousands of games are submitted as designers improve their skills, flex their creative muscles, and try to get their game featured on Game Maker’s Toolkit. You can see (and play!) the past winners here. 

Quick Links


Discord Team Finder Bluesky YouTube

This Year's Theme

The theme for GMTK Game Jam 2025 is "Loop"

How you interpret this theme is entirely up to you.

Perhaps it's about doing the exact same thing over and over again. Perhaps it's about racetracks, or programming instructions, or loop the loops.

You'll get a chance to explain how your game fits the theme when you submit it. And you won't be disqualified if the game doesn't adequately fit the theme.

The Jam is On!

GMTK Game Jam 2025 is now on! You can see exactly how long you have left above - note that the date and time for the jam's end is automatically configured for your time zone.

You can submit your game at any point before the jam ends. There is no submission hour - you simply upload whenever you're ready. Here's how to do that...

How to Submit Your Game

Submitting your game

1. Start by uploading your game to Itch.io through this link.

2. Under pricing, please pick "No payment".

3. Upload the necessary game files. If your game runs in the browser, click here for additional help.

4. Click save & view page.

5. Looks good? Then click "Edit project" at the top of the page, scroll down, and change visibility & access" to "Public".

6. Come back to the GMTK Game Jam page and click "Submit your project".

7. Pick your game from the "Existing project" drop down list, and fill out the questions. 

Checking your game is in the jam

If you did everything right, your game will be in the jam! You can check by writing your game name in the "filter submissions" box on submission feed, or by looking for the following badge in the top right corner of your game's page. 


Please be 100% sure that your game is working correctly, is set to public, and is entered into the jam! You will not be able to submit a game, or change any files, after the jam ends. 

Updating your game

You can update your game at any point before the jam ends by simply uploading new files to your project. You don't need to resubmit - just edit!

Please note that once the jam is closed and the rating period begins you will not be able to update your game. Also - it should go without saying but please do not delete your game during this time. We cannot recover it. 

Common issues

Here are some common problems that jammers run into:

Your game is set to draft, not public. Edit your project from your dashboard and change its visibility to "Public".

You're using the wrong compression type in Unity. Set the compression type to Gzip from Unity's WebGL Player Settings.

If you're uploading an HTML5 build, it must have a file called index.html in the zip archive. Read itch's documentation on browser-based games for more info.

If you're still having trouble getting your game working or submitted into the jam, join the GMTK Discord where we have a dedicated room for troubleshooting these issues!

After the Jam

Once the jam closes, submissions will be completely and truly closed. You will not be able to submit or change your game after this point - no exceptions! 

The jam will then enter a voting period where everyone will get a chance to play and rate the games. If you didn't submit a game you will be given a random list of games to play and rate.

During the voting period your game will be "locked", meaning you can't change any of the files. You can change your Itch page though, if you'd like to swap out screenshots or rewrite the description.

More information about the voting period will be provided once submissions close.

Voting Categories

Voting works a little differently this year.

We'll have five voting criteria - Creativity, Enjoyment, Narrative, Artwork, and Audio.

However, there is no "overall" ranking that combines all of these scores. So please don't feel like your game needs to be perfect in all of these categories!

After the jam is over, Mark will play a subset of the entries - based on all of these scores but weighted in favour of Creativity and Enjoyment.

More information will be shared closer to the jam's start!

Rules

Who Can Join
The GMTK Game Jam is an online game jam, so it's open to anyone, from anywhere, of any age. You can work alone or in teams.
More Details
If you're under the age of 13, a parent or guardian should upload the game.
There is no limit on the number of people per team, and people can be in multiple teams. You can submit more than one game.
Only one person needs to join itch.io and submit the game. However, if others on the team also join itch.io, they can be tagged as contributors. (On your game's itch page, pick "Edit", "More", "Admins", and follow the instructions). 

Game Compatibility
You must upload a digital game to itch.io, which is playable by someone using a Windows PC, with nothing more than a keyboard or mouse. It should not require additional hardware or software to run.
More Details
You can support controllers and other peripherals, as long as keyboard or mouse also works.
You can upload builds for Mac, Linux, Android, etc, as long as you also have a Windows or browser version.
You should not require the user to download additional software from external sources. If your game requires additional files (like DLLs) they should be included in the download and not require separate installation.
The itch upload limit is 1GB. You can compress your game as a ZIP, RAR, or 7Zip file (ZIP is preferred). You can also upload larger files via Butler if you really need to.
You can have online features like multiplayer servers and leaderboards - but files hosted outside of itch.io must not be modified or updated during the voting period - with the only exception being to ensure compliance with itch.io rules (i.e. removing a rulebreaking leaderboard name).

Content Suitability
The game should be suitable for a general audience, so please avoid strong language and excessive gore. And your game should not contain nudity, or hateful language or visuals.

Assets
You may use pre-existing code and can use whatever art and audio assets you have the legal right to use, but you shouldn't make stuff ahead of time that is specifically for this jam. You should credit all external art and audio in your game description. 
More Details
You can use pre-existing code, libraries, and functions. And make use of generic code for things like menus or input.
You can also create things like a blank Unity project, a Github page, or your itch.io profile in advance to save time.
You can use pre-existing sprites, textures, fonts, models, shaders, animations, sound effects, and music - including public domain assets, assets you have purchased, assets you have made yourself, or assets that are (legally-acceptable) derivative works of existing media. 

Generative AI
You must not use generative AI to make art or audio assets for your game, or your Itch.io page. This will lead to your game being disqualified.
More Details
In general, we ask that you do not use artificial intelligence for the GMTK Game Jam. Just make the game yourself, or find someone who can help! However, we are only able to actively police the use of generative AI for art and audio assets.

Voting
When the jam finishes, everyone is invited to play the games and rate them against five criteria: Creativity, Enjoyment, Narrative, Artwork, and Audio. After the jam finishes, Mark will play a selection of the top-rated games and pick his favourites for a video on Game Maker's Toolkit.
More Details
When the jam ends, there's a five-day rating period where uploads to the jam will be locked, meaning you can no longer provide bug fixes, additional versions, or extra levels.  
Please do not put links to other builds (such as "Game jam upload has a bug! Please play this one!”) in your description or comments. This will see you being disqualified from the jam.
If you delete your game during this period, we will not be able to help you and you will be disqualified from the jam.
You can change your game's page during this time, including the thumbnail, description text, screenshots, and page design. You'll want to do this as fast as possible though, as the majority of the ratings happen at the very start of the rating period.
Once the rating period is over, you're free to update your game however you please, charge for it, release it on other platforms - the game is yours, do what you wish with it.
